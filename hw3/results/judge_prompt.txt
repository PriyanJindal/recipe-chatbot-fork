Query and Response: Here's a delicious diabetic-friendly recipe for a **Almond Flour Vanilla Birthday Cake** that serves 2 people. It's low in carbohydrates and uses natural sweeteners to keep the flavor satisfying without the sugar spike.

### Almond Flour Vanilla Birthday Cake

#### Ingredients:
- 1 cup almond flour
- 1/4 cup erythritol (or another sugar substitute of your choice)
- 1/4 tsp baking powder
- 1/4 tsp baking soda
- 1/4 tsp salt
- 2 large eggs
- 2 tbsp unsweetened applesauce
- 1/2 tsp vanilla extract
- 1/4 cup unsweetened almond milk (or any milk alternative)
- Optional: sugar-free frosting or whipped cream for decoration

#### Instructions:

1. **Preheat the Oven:**
   Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a small round cake pan (about 6 inches) or line it with parchment paper.

2. **Mix Dry Ingredients:**
   In a medium mixing bowl, combine the almond flour, erythritol, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Stir these dry ingredients until they are well mixed.

3. **Combine Wet Ingredients:**
   In another bowl, whisk together the eggs, applesauce, vanilla extract, and almond milk until the mixture is smooth and well-blended.

4. **Combine All Ingredients:**
   Gradually add the wet mixture to the dry ingredients, stirring until just combined. Be careful not to over-mix, as this can affect the texture of the cake.

5. **Pour into Pan:**
   Pour the batter into the prepared cake pan. Use a spatula to spread it evenly, smoothing out the top.

6. **Bake:**
   Place the cake in the preheated oven. Bake for about 20-25 minutes or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean. The cake should be lightly golden on top.

7. **Cool the Cake:**
   Once baked, remove the cake from the oven and allow it to cool in the pan for about 10 minutes. Carefully run a knife around the edges to loosen it and then turn it out onto a wire rack to cool completely.

8. **Decoration:**
   Once the cake is completely cool, you can frost it with sugar-free frosting or top it with a dollop of whipped cream if you like. You can also add some fresh berries for a burst of flavor without adding sugar.

9. **Serve:**
   Slice the cake in half to share, and enjoy your delicious and diabetes-friendly birthday treat!

This Almond Flour Vanilla Birthday Cake is delightful and offers a great balance of flavor and nutrition for those watching their sugar intake. Enjoy the celebration!

The recipe replaces traditional sugar with erythritol, a sugar substitute that does not significantly impact blood glucose levels. 5. **Combine with Pasta**: Add the drained pasta to the skillet and toss it in the sauce. If the sauce seems too thick, gradually add some reserved pasta water until you reach your desired consistency. 

6. **Add Cheese**: If using cheese, sprinkle it over the pasta and toss lightly to combine.

7. **Serve**: Divide the Creamy Tomato Basil Pasta between two plates. Garnish with additional fresh basil and, if desired, a sprinkle of extra cheese.

### Quinoa and Spinach Stuffed Bell Peppers

#### Ingredients:
- 2 medium-sized bell peppers (any color)
- 1 cup quinoa, rinsed
- 2 cups vegetable broth (or water)
- 2 cups fresh spinach, roughly chopped
- 1 small onion, finely chopped
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 teaspoon olive oil
- 1 teaspoon dried oregano
- 1 teaspoon paprika
- Salt and pepper to taste
- 1 cup shredded cheese (optional, choose your favorite; for a lighter option, you can use feta)

#### Instructions:

1. **Preheat the Oven:**
   Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).

2. **Prepare the Quinoa:**
   In a medium saucepan, bring the vegetable broth (or water) to a boil. Add the rinsed quinoa, a pinch of salt, and stir. Reduce the heat to low, cover, and let it simmer for about 15 minutes or until all the liquid is absorbed and the quinoa is fluffy. Remove from heat and set aside.

3. **Sauté the Vegetables:**
   While the quinoa is cooking, heat the olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, and sauté until the onion becomes translucent (about 3-5 minutes). Stir in the chopped spinach and continue to cook until the spinach wilts, which will take about 2 minutes.

4. **Combine the Filling:**
   In a large bowl, combine the cooked quinoa, sautéed onion, garlic, and spinach. Add the dried oregano, paprika, salt, and pepper to taste. If you're using cheese, mix half of it into the filling and reserve the rest for topping.

5. **Prepare the Bell Peppers:**
   While you’re mixing the filling, cut the tops off the bell peppers and remove the seeds. You can keep the tops for decoration if you like, or chop them for another use.

6. **Stuff the Peppers:**
   Spoon the quinoa mixture into each bell pepper, packing it lightly but not too tightly. Place the stuffed peppers upright in a baking dish.

7. **Bake:**
   If using, sprinkle the remaining cheese on top of each stuffed pepper. Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil to retain moisture and bake in the preheated oven for 30 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until the peppers are tender and the cheese is melted and bubbly.

8. **Serve:**
   Carefully remove the stuffed peppers from the oven and let them cool slightly before serving. Enjoy warm, garnished with fresh herbs if desired.

This recipe serves 2 people, with each person getting one stuffed pepper.
